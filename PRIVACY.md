# KD CONNECT — Privacy Policy

_Last updated: 2026._

KD CONNECT lets your Windows laptop control your Android phone over your local Wi‑Fi network.

## What data is collected
**None.** KD CONNECT has no accounts, no analytics, no advertising, and no cloud servers.

## What data is processed, and where
- **Input events** (mouse movement, clicks, key presses, gestures) are sent **directly from your
  laptop to your phone** over your local network. They are used only to move the cursor, tap,
  swipe, and type on the phone at that moment. They are **not stored** and **not transmitted to
  anyone else**.
- **Device names / IP addresses** are exchanged on your local network only, to discover and
  connect the two devices. They are not uploaded anywhere.
- **A pairing token** is stored locally on each device so an approved laptop can reconnect
  without asking again. It never leaves your devices.

## Permissions the Android app uses, and why
- **Accessibility service** — to draw the on‑screen cursor and inject taps/swipes/navigation.
  KD CONNECT does **not** read screen content for any purpose other than performing the actions
  you send from the laptop.
- **Input method (keyboard)** — to type the text/keys you send from the laptop into the focused
  field. It has no on‑screen keys and collects nothing.
- **Camera** — only when you tap “Scan desktop QR”, to read the pairing code. No images are
  stored or sent.
- **Network / Wi‑Fi state** — to find and connect to the laptop on the local network.

## Your control
- The phone must **approve** each new laptop before it can control the device.
- You can **Disconnect** at any time, or **Turn off the service** completely.
- Uninstalling the app removes all locally stored data (trusted tokens, settings).
